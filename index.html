Understanding Reiki

What Is Reiki?

Reiki is a Japanese energy healing practice developed by Mikao Usui in 1922. The word itself carries its meaning: Rei (universal, spiritual) and Ki (life force energy) — the same vital energy known as Chi in Chinese medicine or Prana in yogic traditions.

During a session, the practitioner serves as a channel for this universal energy, gently guiding it through light touch or hovering hands placed along the body's energy centers. Reiki doesn't diagnose or prescribe. It works with your body's own intelligence — supporting relaxation, easing tension, and encouraging the natural processes of balance and renewal.

"Reiki is not about fixing what's broken. It's about remembering what's whole."

You don't need to believe in anything for Reiki to work. You don't need to understand it. You just need to be willing to receive — to lie still for an hour and let something quiet happen. Many people feel warmth, gentle tingling, or a deep sense of calm. Some simply fall asleep. All of it is exactly right.
